This Is Karen.

By Jan Diehm , Sara Stoudt , & Amber Thomas

You know Karen. You’ve seen her at Red Lobster, taking in a ball game, stuck in traffic, and walking her dog in Central Park.

But how did Karen become Karen — an entitled middle-aged white woman who needs to speak to the manager? According to baby name data from the Social Security Administration (SSA), Karen first appeared in the female Top 20 in 1941 and spent 30 years there until it dropped out in 1971. By the 1990s, Karen had fallen out of the Top 100.

Chances are you don’t know too many Karens today, but when Karen hit it’s peak at #3 in 1965 Karens were everywhere. It’s this swift decay in popularity that’s made Karen so era defining (like Patricia before her, and Shirley before her) and that’s why the name has found new notoriety as a pejorative meme. Karen has become the perfect caricature of women of a certain age and a certain privilege.

Charting Karen’s trajectory got us thinking: are there other names that are equally as Karen as Karen? First we gathered baby names from 1918–2018. Then we dropped any name that never made it to the Top 20 (to account for popularity) and didn’t appear in the top list at least half of the years, or 50 out of 100 (to account for longevity). That left us with 129 female names and 76 male names (yes, we’re going there too!) to test correlation. Names that have a strong correlation, or above 0.7 were included.
